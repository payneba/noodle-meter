Natural experiment 3 — the weekly rhythm

Day-of-week is orthogonal to the season, so it identifies the effect cleanly. Bars show implied lights-out relative to the weekly average, by the evening the lights went out. Blue evenings precede a commute; red ones don’t.

A single “commute tomorrow” flag is worth +3,489 ± 560 revs (t = +6.2). The household’s commute schedule is recoverable from a hamster wheel.

Caveat. The model assumes the weekly pattern is lights-out timing; statistically it is indistinguishable from any other weekly habit. And the absolute clock times are not identified — trust the offsets.

Verdict. Photoperiod is doing the work. A trend can mimic it in-sample because both fall monotonically from winter to summer — but it fails exactly where the two diverge, and it has no mechanism for the DST step or the weekly cycle.
